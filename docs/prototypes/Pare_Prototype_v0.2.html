A calmer way to reclaim disk space on your Mac. Pare scans, you decide, and anything removed is recoverable for 30 days.

Everything stays on your Mac. Pare runs entirely on-device. No file names, paths, or contents ever leave.

Grant Full Disk Access

To scan everywhere reclaimable space hides — caches, mail attachments, developer artefacts — macOS asks you to enable Full Disk Access for Pare in System Settings.

  • 1. Click Open System Settings below.
  • 2. In Privacy & Security → Full Disk Access, toggle Pare on.
  • 3. Return to Pare — it'll continue automatically.
    • You can also continue in Limited Mode — Pare will scan System Junk only and prompt you again later.

    Help & FAQ

    Common questions about how Pare works. Still stuck? Contact support.

    Will Pare delete something important?

    No — Pare never permanently deletes on its own. Everything you confirm goes to a sandboxed 30-day Recovery Bin first. Protected system paths are excluded at the engine level and cannot be removed even if you try.

    Why does Pare need Full Disk Access?

    Without it, macOS hides most of the locations where reclaimable space accumulates — Mail attachments, Library caches, developer artefacts. You can still use Pare in Limited Mode for System Junk only.

    Does any of my data leave my Mac?

    No. Pare runs entirely on-device. The only network call is a periodic licence check against api.pare.app. Anonymous telemetry is off by default; you can opt in under Settings → Privacy.

    How do I restore something I removed?

    Click Recovery Bin in the sidebar, find the item, and hit Restore. The file returns to its original path. After 30 days it's permanently removed by a background job.

    What's the free vs. Premium difference?

    The free tier reclaims up to 500 MB per calendar month. Premium removes the cap and unlocks the Developer Junk module's deep simulator cleanup. From ₦9,500 / year or $14.99 / year.

    Can I run Pare from the command line?

    Not in v1.0. A `pare` CLI is on the v1.1 roadmap and will use the same helper, so scans and cleanups from the terminal stay perfectly in sync with the app.
